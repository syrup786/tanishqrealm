What is a Surprise Diamond?

A surprise diamond is typically set below the collet (or sometimes under the main setting), often hidden from plain view. It is placed in such a way that it is not visible when looking at the piece from the top but can be seen from a different angle or when the jewelry is handled closely.

When a single rough diamond is crafted into two diamonds, will both have the same color and clarity, weight etc?

No, they can vary. The two diamonds come from different sections of the same rock, which will have different quality parameters.

Stone Settings

Prong

  • Usually with either 4 or 6 prongs are bent over the girdle of the gemstone or diamond.
  • Prong set diamonds receives maximum exposure to light from top to bottom and maximizes its beauty.

Bezel

  • A bezel is a thin metal strip that wraps around a gem to hold it in place.
  • Strong and secure setting, avoids snagging on clothes, best for regular wear.
  • Ideal setting for ____ stone as it look richer and darker in colour.

Channel

  • Stones are suspended between two bars or strips of metal called channels.
  • Most preferred settins for baguettes, princess cut and emerald cut as it offers more protection to the girdle.
  • Stone damage is very less.

Pave

  • (pah-VAY) setting is a modified version of prong setting.
  • The diamonds set on the metal with the help of tiny beads, which are pulled up from the metal and pushed against the diamond.

Invisible

  • One of the most difficult setting methods.
  • The stones are positioned in such a manner that metal is not visible in between stones.
  • Gives the appearance of an uninterrupted and continuous surface.

Flush

  • The gems are set directly into the jewellery item.
  • The top of the gem (the "table") in even with the surface of the metal.
  • Besides being beautiful, the flush setting is very secure.

Pressure

  • Small stones are set together in a cluster, the center stone is kept one level down than the other stones without any other prongs.

Nick

  • The stone is set in a collet of reflective and brightly polished metal.
  • Then the metal edges are cut or shaped, so that when bent around the stone, they create the illusion of being a part of the stone, enhances the apparent size of stone.

Napoli

  • It is a channel setting.
  • Same sized diamonds set with a prong in between.
  • Creates an illusion of a single large stone.

Emero

  • Small ____ and round/princess diamonds set together to create a ____ emerald cut diamond.

Tension

  • The entire band of the ring acts as two large prongs to hold the stone in place, usually with small groves that fit around the stone's girdle.
  • ____ more light, offering more brilliance and sparkle.

Bouquet

  • It is a ____ of one big diamond in the center surrounded by several (9-11) smaller diamonds on the side placed at an angle to create the look of a diamond table.

Composite

  • It is a cluster setting with one big diamond in the center surrounded by several (9-12) diamonds on the side at the same level in pave setting (round head prongs).
  • Smaller diamonds appears one big Diamond.

Hallmarking and HUID

About Hallmarking

  • Mandated by the BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards)
  • Compulsory for Gold Jewellery, from June 2021 for all jewelers across India.
  • Permitted in four categories: 24K, 22K, 18K and 14K gold products.
  • Jewellery weight less than 2 grams is exempted from Hallmark.
  • Kundan-Polki and Glass-Kundan Jewellery is also exempted, whereas in Tanishq all Glass-Kundan and Kundan-Polki Jewellery is Hallmarked.
  • Authenticity of Hallmark can be checked at the 'BIS Care App' provided by the Bureau of Indian Standards.

Exemption of Hallmarking for Jewellery items below specified weights, effective from - 21st November 2024.

  • Single-Item Jewellery: Products with a single item under one barcode and gross weight below 1.950 grams. Example: Finger rings, pendants.
  • Two-Item Jewellery: Products with two items under one barcode and gross weight below 3.500 grams. Example: Earrings, hoops.
  • Multi-Item Jewellery: Products with more than two items under one barcode, even if their gross weight is below the above thresholds, must undergo mandatory Hallmarking. Example: Jewellery sets.

Three symbols

  • The BIS logo (triangle shaped)
  • The purity of gold (as 22K916, 18K750, 14K585)
  • 6-digit alpha-numeric code, also known as HUID number

Exempted Categories

  • Jewellery below 2gms
  • Silver Platinum Jewellery
  • Gold Thread, Plate, sheet, foil, rod, wire, strip, tube or coin have been exempted
  • Some exemption to export Jewellery

Why is there a difference between SafeGold Rate and Tanishq rate?

The Gold Rate in SafeGold is the price of Raw Gold that is being traded in the commodity markets which is influenced by various macroeconomic factors including global commodity pricing and Rupee valuation against USD. This Gold rate is not the selling gold rate of a finished product. There are operational costs involved even after raw gold is purchased from the market to convert the gold for amenability to make jewellery and these processing costs are why the Gold Rate of all jewelers (not just Tanishq) differs from the commodity rates. In addition, the SafeGold rate varies based on the time of the day, as there are intra-day price movements in the commodity market as well.

Can a customer enroll into RGA via E-Mail consent?

RGA enrolment can be done via E-Mail consent only by existing customers whose KYC is already verified by the company.

  • As per customer's request, the store shall send mail from Store Mail ID the brochure soft copy & plan details + T&Cs
  • Customer can then send a reply to the mail from his mail ID with the consent as follows: “I am interested to enrolling in the RGA scheme with enrollment amount of Rs........./- per month and I acknowledge that I have read and do hereby accept the terms and conditions of RGA scheme.”
  • Along with the mail, customer also needs to send his/her Valid ID Proof & PAN Card in case of enrolment amount greater than Rs.18,000/-
  • Then store shall send the Airpay / Razorpay link to the customer. Stores can share the virtual RTGS as well.
  • For each email-based enrollment store has to capture in the remarks field "Email-based enrollment“ in GHS Portal.
  • OTP is mandatory for RGA Scheme enrollment through this process.
  • It's mandatory to create the RGA on the same day in the system with the original payment mode used by the customer , and the same day the details shall be updated in the Shared-Form , and ABM's are responsible for 100% compliance w.r.t. the remote RGA account opening.

How many RGA accounts can be clubbed together & who are allowed to club?

A maximum of 5-RGA accounts can be clubbed together. Clubbing is possible for multiple accounts opened by same customer or by Husband-Wife-Children. Clubbing can be done with different Tiers also (Accordingly MC/UCP benefit to be given based on Credit Note Value). No other clubbing is allowed.

Can customers pre-mature/redeem their RGA accounts before 6-Months?

Yes, he/she can purchase any jewellery for minimum grammage/value of RGA Credit note. Customer will get only Gold rate benefit & no RGA benefit will be given. Any ongoing offer can be given.

Can customers get refund of the RGA amount if they want to withdraw?

RGA does not allow any refund of cash. Customers has to mandatorily purchase any jewellery (NOT GOLD COIN) for the grammage accumulated - Plain /value accumulated - Studded.

If the customer has tried to make RGA instalment payment on last day of the grace period but failed and instalment gets lapsed. What should we do?

Can customers purchase both Studded & Plain products in a single RGA redemption?

Yes. Please make use of RGA Calculator Advanced V3.0 to merge both Studded & Plain products in single RGA redemption.

Can customers make their RGA instalment payments through ACH or Cheque?

ACH & cheque payments are not allowed for RGA.

How will the RGA discount for Plain Gold group/CS & Studded Group (GS, HVS, Solitaire) be calculated?

Plain Gold group, Colourstone, Platinum Bi-Metal(Plain):
MC Discount on the RGA Credit Note value generated on the day of redemption. Customer needs to purchase minimum grammage accumulated to redeem the RGA account.

Studded Group(GS, HVS, Solitaire, Platinum Bi-Metal(Studded)):
UCP discount on the RGA Credit note value generated on the day of redemption.

Plain group & Colourstone:
RGA benefit needs to be passed on as usual.
